Yin Minn Pa Pa

Specially Appointed Associate Professor
Yokohama National University

I study cyberattacks, how attackers operate, and the online ecosystems behind them. I observe real-world attacks through honeypots and internet-scale monitoring, and investigate cybercriminal activity across the web, blockchain and social media, including hidden communities on the dark web. I also explore how AI, particularly large language models, can help analyze and understand cyber threats at scale.

What I work on

Research interests

Observe real-world attacks → Understand the cybercriminals behind them → Transform evidence into actionable intelligence → Intervene and measure real-world impact

Cyberattack Observation & Measurement

What attacks are happening?

Observe real-world attacks through honeypots, Internet-scale measurement, and infrastructure monitoring.

1

Cybercrime Ecosystems & Threat Actors

Who is behind them, and how do they operate?

Investigate cybercriminal actors, communities, markets, and their behavior across the open web, social media, and underground ecosystems.

2

Cyber Threat Intelligence & AI

How can we connect the evidence and turn it into actionable intelligence at scale?

Correlate attack observations, threat-actor activity, and other data sources, using AI where appropriate, to generate actionable intelligence for prevention and countermeasures.

3

Intervention & Impact Measurement

Can intelligence lead to measurable disruption?

Translate research findings into interventions such as abuse reporting, then monitor what happens afterward to measure their effectiveness and understand how adversaries respond.
