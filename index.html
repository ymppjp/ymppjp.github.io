Yin Minn Pa Pa

Specially Appointed Associate Professor
Yokohama National University

I study cyberattacks, how attackers operate, and the online ecosystems behind them. I observe real-world attacks through honeypots and internet-scale monitoring, and investigate cybercriminal activity across the web, blockchain and social media, including hidden communities on the dark web. I also explore how AI, particularly large language models, can help analyze and understand cyber threats at scale.

What I work on

Research interests

I watch attacks happen, investigate how attackers operate, and use methods ranging from honeypots and internet-scale measurement to AI to understand cyber threats.

Cyberattack Observation & Measurement

What attacks are happening?

Observing real-world attacks through honeypots and internet-scale measurement from IoT botnets and exposed infrastructure to emerging attack activity.

Cybercrime Ecosystems & Threat Actors

Who is behind them, and how do they operate?

Investigating how cybercriminals operate across ransomware groups, underground forums, social media, and darknet markets and how these ecosystems evolve and respond to disruption.

AI-Assisted Cyber Threat Intelligence

How can we analyze threats at scale and turn insights into actionable countermeasures?

Using AI and large language models to analyze cyber threats at scale transforming large volumes of attack data into actionable insights for effective countermeasures and prevention.

Malware & Sandbox Evasion Analysis

How do attackers and malware avoid detection?

Studying how malware and attackers evade detection and analysis and developing methods to better observe and understand evasive threats.
